Major overhauls

Recently I've spent a lot of time researching padding and margins and proper usage of both in the modern Internet age, and I've realized that my site will need some major overhauls. I've developed a much better understanding of how I should be structuring content and realized that I've relied in the past on double-stacking margins and other frowned-upon practices. Mobile support will continue to be a long-term goal while I get the styling figured out.

Another issue is my usage of sections and paragraphs. I remember my English teacher saying that each paragraph should have at least three or four sentences and that coherent ideas should span paragraphs. A paragraph is a short block of sentences to string together one part of a much larger idea. Maybe I should just do away with p tags altogether and stick with section tags? I want to simplify the site as much as possible while it's in this interim phase and this may save me some headache down the line, or cause even more. It's so early in the process that I may just have to default to what I've been doing already so I don't have to go back and make edits to already-finished content.

I feel like I'll encounter many more of these tough decisions as I continue to build this website. I think what I'll do is add a border or some other marker to visually differentiate a section from a paragraph, but keep the same padding and margins to avoid confusion.
